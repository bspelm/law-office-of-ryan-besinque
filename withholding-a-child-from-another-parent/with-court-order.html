Is Withholding a Child from Another Parent Allowed In New York?

When parents divorce, there are many decisions that must be made regarding their children. One of the biggest issues is custody. Unless both parties agree otherwise, the courts will award legal and physical custody to either or both parents. If a non-custodial parent violates those rights, the court could take various courses of action. In this article, we will explore whether withholding a child from another parent with court order is allowed in New York.

Generally speaking, the courts in New York prefer to award joint legal and physical custody to both parents. This allows both parents to make important decisions about their children and ensure that both are involved in their lives. Despite the preference to give both parents equal custody rights, courts can award sole custody to one parent if they feel it is in the best interests of the child. While both parents should try to work together to decide what is in the best interests of their children, if that is not possible then courts will determine custody and visitation arrangements on their behalf. Parents found to be withholding a child from another parent with court order may face adjustments to these custody arrangements to protect the child’s well-being.
